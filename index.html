Chordotron - Quick Start

What is Chordotron?

Chordotron is a web-based tool to quickly prototype, play, and experiment with chord progressions. You can input chords directly, use scale degrees, or trigger chords live with your keyboard.

1. Inputting Your Progression / Chords

Choose your input method:

  • üéµ Chord Names: Enter standard chord notation for sequential playback.
    • Example: E(8) C#m(8) A(8) B(8)
    • (4) denotes the duration in beats (optional, defaults to beats per measure).
    • Slash chords like G/B are supported.
  • üéº Scale Degrees: Enter Roman numerals relative to a chosen key and mode for sequential playback.
    • Example: I(2) V(2) vi(2) iii(2) IV(2) I(2) IV(2) V(2) in D Major.
    • Set Song Key and Mode.
  • üéπ Live Playing: Assign up to 12 chords to keyboard keys (1-9, 0, -, =) for immediate playback.
    • Enter a chord (e.g., Am7, G/B) into one of the 12 input slots.
    • Press and hold the corresponding key on your computer keyboard (1, 2, ..., 0, -, =) to play the chord. The chord will sound as long as the key is held.
    • In this mode, BPM, Time Signature, Loop, Play/Stop, and Metronome controls are disabled.

Common chord qualities: m, maj, M, dim, aug, sus4, sus2, 7, maj7, m7, dim7, o, √∏, +, and extensions (9, 11, 13).

2. Setting Parameters (‚öôÔ∏è)

  • ‚è±Ô∏è Timing (Chord/Degree Modes Only):
    • BPM: Beats Per Minute.
    • Time Signature: e.g., 4/4, 3/4, 6/8. Affects default chord duration and metronome.
  • üéπ Synth (All Modes):
    • Oscillator: Waveform (Sine, Square, Sawtooth, Triangle).
    • Envelope (ADSR): Attack, Decay, Sustain, Release times for the sound's shape.
    • Gain: Volume of the synth sound itself.
  • üîä Master & Metronome:
    • Master Gain (All Modes): Overall output volume.
    • Metronome Vol Adj (Chord/Degree Modes Only): Adjusts metronome volume relative to master gain.
  • ‚ÜîÔ∏è Pitch Range (All Modes):
    • Range Start & Length: Defines the allowed MIDI note range for voiced chords. Chords are voiced to fit within these boundaries.

3. Playback Controls (Footer - Chord/Degree Modes Only)

  • ‚ñ∂Ô∏è Play / ‚èπÔ∏è Stop (Spacebar): Starts or stops sequential playback.
  • üîÅ Loop: Toggles looping of the progression.
  • ü•Å Metro: Toggles the audible metronome click.
  • Chord Display: Shows previous, current, and next chords.
  • Beat Indicators: Visual cue for beats.
  • (Note: These controls are disabled in Live Playing mode.)

4. Keyboard (Middle Section - All Modes)

The piano keyboard visualizes the notes being played for the current chord. In Chord/Degree modes, it also highlights the selected Pitch Range. In Live Playing mode, the range defined in Parameters is used for voicing.

5. File Operations (Top Bar - All Modes)

  • üìÑ New: Resets all settings and inputs to default.
  • üìÅ Load: Load a previously saved song/settings (.json file).
  • üíæ Save: Save your current inputs and all parameter settings as a .json file.
  • ‚ùì Help: Shows this guide again.

Tips & Shortcuts

  • Spacebar: Play/Stop (Chord/Degree modes).
  • L key: Toggle Loop (Chord/Degree modes).
  • M key: Toggle Metronome (Chord/Degree modes).
  • 1 to = keys (Hold): Trigger assigned chords (Live Playing mode).
  • You can play a selection of your input by highlighting text in the Chord/Degree input area before pressing Play.
